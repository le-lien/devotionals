Oswald Chambers

Devotional: My Utmost For The Highest

Oswald Chambers (1874–1917) was a Scottish Baptist evangelist, Bible teacher, and military chaplain best known for the devotional classic My Utmost for His Highest. His Christ-centered preaching, emphasis on absolute surrender to God, and posthumously published writings have made him one of the most widely read Christian devotional authors of the twentieth century and beyond.

Early Life & Education

  • Born 24 July 1874 in Aberdeen, Scotland, into a devout Baptist family; his father was a minister involved in evangelistic work and temperance ministry.
  • Converted to Christ as a teenager (around age 15–16) and soon became involved in evangelism among the poor in London, already noted for his deep spirituality.
  • Gifted in art and music, he studied at the National Art Training School (now the Royal College of Art) in London and later at the University of Edinburgh before sensing a call to ministry.
  • Left his art studies to attend Dunoon College, a small theological training school near Glasgow, where he later also taught.

Ministry & Holiness Teaching

  • Influenced by the Holiness Movement and the Pentecostal League of Prayer, he developed a strong emphasis on surrender, holiness, and a deeper life with God.
  • Travelled widely as a preacher and teacher in Britain, the United States, and Japan, gaining a reputation as an insightful and challenging Bible expositor.
  • Married Gertrude “Biddy” Hobbs in 1910; she was a highly skilled shorthand writer who recorded his talks verbatim, later turning them into the books that bear his name.

Bible Training College & War Service

  • In 1911 he founded and led the Bible Training College in Clapham, London, where students were trained for missionary and Christian service in an atmosphere of prayer, study, and practical ministry.
  • With the outbreak of World War I, he closed the college in 1915 and served as a YMCA chaplain to British and Commonwealth troops in Egypt, where he taught, counselled soldiers, and led Bible studies in the camp at Zeitoun near Cairo.
  • Known among the troops for his warm personal concern, clear teaching, and willingness to offer spiritual and practical help without pressure.

Death & Posthumous Writings

  • In October 1917 he suffered acute appendicitis; after an emergency operation he died on 15 November 1917 in Cairo from complications, aged 43, and was buried there with military honours.
  • Before his death he had prepared his first book manuscript, but the majority of his published works were compiled afterward by Biddy from her shorthand notes of his sermons and lectures.
  • His best-known work, My Utmost for His Highest, a 365-day devotional drawn from his talks, has remained continuously in print, been translated into dozens of languages, and become a classic of evangelical spirituality.

Legacy

  • Chambers is remembered as a deeply God-centered teacher whose life motto was to be “abandoned to God,” calling believers to give their utmost for Christ’s highest purposes.
  • His writings continue to shape Christians worldwide, particularly in the areas of discipleship, holiness, and wholehearted devotion to Jesus.

Max Lucado

Devotional: Grace For The Moment & others

Max Lucado (born 11 January 1955) is an American pastor, best-selling Christian author, and speaker known for his warm, story-rich writing style that makes biblical truth accessible to everyday people. He serves as Teaching Minister at Oak Hills Church in San Antonio, Texas, and leads UpWords, The Teaching Ministry of Max Lucado, which aims to help people take one step closer to Jesus Christ.

Early Life & Education

  • Born in San Angelo, Texas, and raised in the small West Texas town of Andrews, the youngest of four children of Jack and Thelma Lucado; his father worked in the oil fields and his mother was a nurse.
  • Grew up in a churchgoing home but drifted toward rebellion in his teens; after a particularly heavy night of partying, he began questioning the direction of his life and turned more seriously to God.
  • Studied at Abilene Christian University, earning a B.A. in Mass Communication and later a master’s degree in Biblical Studies, where a required Bible course and a mission trip shifted his plans from law to ministry.

Call to Ministry & Mission Work

  • First served as an associate minister at Central Church of Christ in Miami, Florida, working with a singles ministry and writing for the church newsletter; these newsletter articles became the seed of his future books.
  • In the early 1980s, moved with his wife Denalyn to Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, as full-time missionaries, planting churches and deepening his passion for communicating the gospel to ordinary people.
  • Returned to the United States after several years in Brazil, in part to be closer to family following his father’s illness and death.

Oak Hills Church

  • In 1988, Lucado joined Oak Hills Church (then Oak Hills Church of Christ) in San Antonio, Texas, where he has served for decades, helping lead its growth into a large, non-denominational congregation.
  • After years as Senior Minister, he transitioned to the role of Teaching Minister due to health concerns, continuing to preach regularly and shape the church’s teaching ministry.

Writing & Media Ministry

  • Author of more than 40 books for adults and children, with combined sales well over 100 million copies worldwide; his works include He Chose the Nails, Just Like Jesus, Grace for the Moment, Anxious for Nothing, You Are Special (children’s), and many others.
  • Often described as writing “books for people who don’t read books,” combining simple language, vivid stories, and pastoral warmth to communicate themes of God’s love, grace, and hope for the hurting and discouraged.
  • Through UpWords, his teaching ministry distributes daily devotionals, podcasts, videos, and radio programs focused on helping listeners grow closer to Christ in everyday life.
  • Widely recognized in evangelical circles, he has been called “America’s Pastor” and “Best Preacher in America” by major Christian and popular media outlets.

Recent Ministry & Ongoing Influence

  • Continues to preach at Oak Hills Church and speak at conferences and special events, while also occasionally serving in interim teaching roles at other churches, all with a focus on encouraging people to trust Jesus in anxious and uncertain times.
  • His books, sermons, and media resources remain widely used by churches and individual believers around the world for personal devotions, small groups, and outreach, shaping contemporary Christian spirituality with a strong emphasis on God’s kindness and grace.

Derek Prince

Devotional: Declaring God's Word

Derek Prince (1915–2003) was a British-born international Bible teacher, pastor, missionary, and theologian, known for his clear, systematic teaching of Scripture and his deep Christian faith. He authored over 100 books and produced extensive teaching materials that have been translated into more than 100 languages and continue to influence millions of Christians worldwide.

Early Life & Conversion

  • Born 14 August 1915 in Bangalore, India, into a British military family.
  • Educated at Eton and at King’s College, Cambridge, where he specialised in ancient and modern philosophy and studied classical and biblical languages, including Hebrew and Aramaic.
  • Raised in the Anglican tradition, he became an atheist during his university years before encountering Jesus while serving in the British Army during World War II.
  • On 31 July 1941, while reading the Bible as a “philosophical work,” he experienced a powerful conversion and became convinced that Jesus is alive and that the Bible is the Word of God.

World War II & Calling

  • Served as a non-combatant in the Royal Army Medical Corps, spending much of his service in North Africa.
  • While stationed in the Middle East, he devoted himself to the study of Scripture.
  • In 1944 he sensed a clear call from God to be “a teacher of the Scriptures, in truth and faith and love…for many,” a word he later saw fulfilled as his ministry expanded globally.

Family & Missionary Work

  • Married Danish missionary Lydia Christensen in 1946 in Jerusalem, becoming father to her eight adopted girls; later they also adopted a Kenyan baby.
  • Lived through the founding of the modern State of Israel, then ministered in England (including open-air preaching at Hyde Park), and later served as missionaries in Kenya from 1957, seeing significant spiritual fruit. Lydia died in 1975.
  • In 1978 he married Ruth Baker, an American single mother of three adopted children. Together they launched the radio programme “Today with Derek Prince”, which spread his teaching worldwide in many languages.
  • Ruth died in 1998. In his grief Derek publicly reaffirmed his trust in God, a decision he described as pivotal for the rest of his life.

Ministry Impact

  • From the late 1960s, Derek became a leading Bible teacher in the Charismatic movement, travelling widely and preaching with evident power and authority.
  • In 1971 he founded what became Derek Prince Ministries (originally Derek Prince Publications) in Fort Lauderdale to distribute his messages.
  • Today, the ministry operates in more than 45 countries, continuing his mission of “resourcing the spiritually hungry.”

Death & Legacy

  • Derek Prince died of natural causes on 24 September 2003 in Jerusalem, aged 88, after a period of declining health.
  • His tombstone describes him as “A Teacher of the Scriptures / In Truth and Faith and Love / Which are in Christ Jesus for Many,” echoing the prophetic call he received in 1944.
  • Near the end of his life he said the Church’s greatest need was “serious Bible teachers,” and many still regard him as one of the most significant Bible teachers of the 20th century.

Paul Tripp

Devotional: New Morning Mercies

Paul David Tripp (born 12 November 1950) is an American Reformed pastor, award-winning author, biblical counselor, and international conference speaker. He is the founder and president of Paul Tripp Ministries, a nonprofit organization whose mission is to connect the transforming power of Jesus Christ to everyday life through books, video series, conferences, and online resources.

Early Life & Education

  • Born in Toledo, Ohio, and raised in a Christian home where family worship and church life were central, experiences that later shaped his focus on real-life applications of the gospel.
  • Studied at Columbia Bible College (now Columbia International University), then completed a Master of Divinity at Reformed Episcopal Seminary and a Doctor of Ministry in Biblical Counseling at Westminster Theological Seminary.

Ministry & Counseling Work

  • Began ministry as a pastor and church planter, also helping to start a Christian school, combining preaching, discipleship, and practical care for families.
  • Served for many years as a counselor and faculty member with the Christian Counseling & Educational Foundation (CCEF) and taught biblical counseling and pastoral ministry at Westminster Theological Seminary, as well as lecturing at other institutions such as Southern Baptist Theological Seminary.
  • Frequently speaks at conferences around the world on topics such as marriage, parenting, suffering, leadership, and Christian living.

Paul Tripp Ministries

  • In 2006 he founded Paul Tripp Ministries, a nonprofit organization focused on producing books, devotionals, video curricula, podcasts, and live events that show how the gospel intersects with the realities of everyday life.
  • The ministry’s stated mission is “connecting the transforming power of Jesus Christ to everyday life,” a phrase that summarizes the heart of his teaching and writing.

Writing & Key Themes

  • Author of more than 30 books and video series on Christian living, including influential works such as Instruments in the Redeemer’s Hands, Age of Opportunity, What Did You Expect?, Parenting, Dangerous Calling, and the devotional New Morning Mercies.
  • His teaching emphasizes how the grace of God in Christ reshapes identity, relationships, and responses to suffering, with a strong focus on the heart motivations behind behavior rather than mere external change.
  • Widely used in churches, seminaries, and counseling contexts, his resources aim to help pastors, parents, and everyday believers live with honest self-awareness, repentance, and hope in a broken world.

Personal Life

  • Paul is married to Luella Tripp; they live in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, and have four grown children.

Through his preaching, counseling, books, and digital content, Paul David Tripp has become a widely recognized voice in contemporary evangelicalism, encouraging Christians to bring every area of life under the liberating lordship of Jesus Christ.

Smith Wigglesworth

Devotional: Smith Wigglesworth Devotional

Smith Wigglesworth (1859–1947), often called the “Apostle of Faith”, was a British Pentecostal evangelist known for his bold preaching, uncompromising trust in God’s Word, and a healing ministry that deeply influenced early Pentecostalism. Born into poverty in Yorkshire, England, he rose from an illiterate plumber to a globally recognized minister whose life and sermons have inspired generations of Christians to live by faith and expect the supernatural work of the Holy Spirit.

Early Life & Conversion

  • Born on 8 June 1859 in Menston, West Yorkshire, England, to a very poor family; he began working as a child in fields and factories to help support the household.
  • Though his parents were not committed Christians, his Wesleyan Methodist grandmother took him to church, where as a young boy he experienced a personal awareness of Jesus&rsquo love and decided to follow Christ.
  • As he grew, he was involved with several denominations and movements, including the Methodist church, the Church of England, Baptist believers, the Salvation Army, and later the Plymouth Brethren, all of which helped shape his devotion to prayer, evangelism, and holiness.

Trade, Marriage & Call to Ministry

  • Trained and worked as a plumber in Bradford, combining his trade with active evangelism, especially among poor and neglected children.
  • Moved to Liverpool for a season, where he used his wages to feed and clothe destitute children and led many of them to faith in Christ.
  • Married Mary Jane “Polly” Featherstone in 1882, a strong Salvation Army preacher who helped teach him to read; Smith later said he primarily read the Bible and no other books.
  • Together they opened a small mission in a poor area of Bradford. Polly preached while Smith prayed, supported the family through plumbing, and gradually grew in confidence and calling as a minister.

Baptism in the Holy Spirit & Healing Ministry

  • In 1907, during meetings led by Anglican vicar A. A. Boddy in Sunderland, Smith received the baptism in the Holy Spirit with speaking in tongues, an experience he believed unlocked a new power and fluency in his preaching.
  • After personally experiencing divine healing, he began to pray for the sick, and reports of dramatic healings spread quickly—including testimonies of people being raised from the dead, deliverance from terminal illnesses, and other notable miracles.
  • His ministry was marked by very direct, sometimes physically vigorous ways of praying, but his central message was simple: believe God’s Word, stand on the promises of Scripture, and act in faith.

International Influence

  • From the 1910s onward, Wigglesworth travelled widely across the United Kingdom, Europe, North America, Australia, New Zealand, and other regions, preaching salvation, faith, and healing in evangelistic campaigns.
  • He held credentials for a time with the Assemblies of God and became one of the best-known early Pentecostal healing evangelists, helping to shape the emerging Pentecostal movement worldwide.
  • Messages from his sermons were later compiled into influential books such as Ever Increasing Faith and Faith That Prevails, which continue to be widely read and translated.

Character, Teaching Emphases & Legacy

  • Known for his intense, straightforward personality, he emphasized four simple principles: read the Word of God, let it consume you, believe it, and act on it.
  • Despite limited formal education, he cultivated a deep life of prayer, fasting, compassion for the poor, and a childlike trust in God’s goodness and power.
  • Smith Wigglesworth died on 12 March 1947, but his example of radical faith and expectation of the miraculous remains a major inspiration within Pentecostal and Charismatic Christianity today.
